top-level > top-level > Operational Analysis > Initial Requirements Set > TL-TOP-001 - Top Level Container Contains all the top level requirements

The following requirements are defined at the top level to indicate the needed functionality of the satellite.

Requirements

ID Name Description
TL-ENV-001System Survives Launch Loads

Ensure the system as a whole can endure the launch loads from vibration and shock.

Perform testing to NASA GEVS GSFC-STD-7000 standard with swept sine, random and repeated swept sine vibration.

GSFC-STD-7000 Standard

TL-PAY-003Provide Science Payload

A STEM outreach science payload - most likely a camera imaging earth.

TL-PAY-002Provide AREDN Emergency Communication Node

Support the emergency communication amateur radio community with a fixed GEO asset.

TL-INT-003Manned Flight Compatible

The satellite should be compatible with manned flight standards to faciliate possible ISS launch.

TL-FUN-003Raising to GEO

Capability to raise from deployment in GTO to GEO within 4 weeks.

TL-FUN-001Three year lifetime

All design decisions based upon a minimum three year opertational lifetime.

TL-PAY-001Provide DVB-S2X Transceiver

Provide capability for bi-directional high throughput DVB-S2X communication.

Implement a ETSI DVB-S2X regnerative communication capability.  Support the adaptive ACM codecs for maximum link efficiency.  The 5 GHz (uplink) and 10 GHz (downlink) amateur radio bands will be used.

Target SNR TBC.

ETSI EN 302 307-2

 

 

TL-DES-001Component Derating to ECSS

Where possible design to component derating as defined in ECSS-Q-ST-30-11C.

TL-FUN-004Station Keeping

Capability to maintain orbit within TBD.

TL-FUN-006End of Life Orbit Management

An end of life graveyard orbit or deorbiting ability to be included.

TL-ENV-003Thermal Management

Adequate thermal management required to deal with GEO conditions.

TL-INT-001Compatible with Standard POD Deployers

The satellite must be compatible with industry standard POD deployers.

TL-INT-005Completley Unpowered During Launch

To comply with the launch provider the satellite must be completely unpowered during launch.

TL-INT-004Non-activity 30 Minutes Post Deployment

To meet launch provider regulations the satellite must be in hibernation for 30 minutes after deployment.

TL-INT-006Launch Vehicle EMC Compliance

The satellite must be able to meet launch provider EMC requirements - verfication may be neccesary.

TL-FUN-002Safe Mode Implemented

A safe mode exists which is entered after anomilous conditions, ie. battery voltge drops too low.

TL-INT-002Survive 6+ Month Storage Time

The satellite must be able to stay stored in flight configuration in POD for 6+ months with no loss of capability upon deployment.

TL-ENV-002Radiation Tolerant

Is designed to withstand three years in GEO - aim for FIT longer than a day.

TL-FUN-005Angular Pointing Accuracy

The angular pointing accuracy at the center point of the earth should be less than TBD.
